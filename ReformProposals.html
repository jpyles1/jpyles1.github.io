Reform Proposals

The ECPA embodies many important and useful protections but has changed a lot since 1986. ECPA regulates when eletronic communications
can be intercepted, monitored, or reviewed by third party, making its a crime to get this communication unless they have a court order or an
exception to ECPA. The adoption of cloud computing offers many benefits but mke the need for reform more urgent. Since most communication
is stored on a home server its protected by the fourth amendment, this does not work for remote servers.

Current Proposals

Email Privacy Act of 2016

Representative Kevin Yoder introduced this act in 2015. This proposal includes an extension of the warrant requirement to communications
stored for more than 180 days and a notice before email searchers. This act was passed in the house in 2016 but left out the notice requirement.

Emergency Authorization Provision

Senator Jeff Sessions proposed an emergency disclosure rule to the act which would give an exemption to the warrant requirements in times of
claimed emergency. Under the current act, companies are allowed to voluntarily make emergency disclosures, but the amendment would make it mandatory.

Electronic Communications Transactional Records

Senator Lindsey Graham proposed a "fix" to ECPA which would grant the FBI access to browsing history and location information with
a National Security Letter (NSL). These letters are issued directly by local FBI offices without any court approval or supervision.
